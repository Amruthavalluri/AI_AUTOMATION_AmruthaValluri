# AI Fundamentals Notes

## AI (Artificial Intelligence)
AI enables machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as reasoning, learning, and decision-making.

## Machine Learning (ML)
A subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data instead of being explicitly programmed.

## Deep Learning (DL)
A subset of ML that uses neural networks with multiple layers to solve complex problems.

## Generative AI
Generative AI creates new content such as text, code, images, audio, and documents based on prompts.

Examples:
- ChatGPT
- GitHub Copilot

## Large Language Models (LLMs)
LLMs are AI models trained on massive amounts of text to understand and generate human-like language.

Examples:
- GPT
- Gemini
- Claude

## Tokens
Tokens are small pieces of text processed by an LLM.
A prompt and response are both measured in tokens.

## Context Window
The context window is the maximum number of tokens an AI model can process in a single conversation.
A larger context window allows the model to remember more information but does not eliminate hallucinations.

## Temperature
Temperature controls randomness in AI responses.

- Low (0.0–0.3): Accurate and consistent
- Medium (0.4–0.7): Balanced
- High (0.8–1.0): More creative

## Hallucinations
Hallucinations occur when AI generates incorrect or invented information.
Always verify AI-generated code with official documentation.
